As a long time cinephile, I've also watched a fair share of television. I am not sure if there is a term for that though (telephile, maybe?). In that time I've seen most of the prestige and uber-popular shows of my generation and the generation before. _The Sopranos, Mad Men, The Wire, Breaking Bad_, etc. I've seen them all and several are in my all time favorites list. I have watched and rewatched several of them to the point where they are a major part of how I judge other television shows and most movies. Until about 24 hours ago, I had one big blind spot: ABC's smash hit helmed by [Damon Lindelof](https://www.imdb.com/name/nm0511541/?ref_=tt_ov_wr), _Lost_.

I've always had two really good reasons to not watch _Lost_

1. **The length** - Watching a traditional network show with around 22 episodes a season and 44 minute episodes has become exhausting. All the prestige shows I mentioned above were made in the **"premium mold"** where the seasons are generally half the length of the traditional method. This makes for tighter storytelling and higher production value. This is not to say that I don't enjoy any traditional shows, _Buffy the Vampire Slayer_ is in my pantheon of great TV.
2. **The reaction at the time** - I never watched _Lost_ in its initial run but it would be impossible not to hear people talk about it or even know a few spoilers if you were dialed in to popular culture during the years that it aired. I remember the initial craze but I also remember when viewers cooled on the show and some even went as far as to say that it fell off in quality and the finale was terrible. I try not to let people's reviews cloud my judgement but when it comes to watching 119 episodes of a show, I have to proceed with caution.

## Why watch _Lost_ in 2021?

It is all thanks to [commentary YouTuber Drew Gooden](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=97Z3qSSbwkY). He made a video about the show praising it and saying that the initial reaction was overblown. I took his word for it and decided to pull the trigger. Boy am I happy that I did.

## Is _Lost_ any good?

Yes. _Lost_ is very good. I would even say that it is **great**. The first thing you need to know about _Lost_ is that it is no miracle of story-telling. I do not find the story to be bad but it is no where as deep as the shows listed earlier. It has lots of twists and mystery boxes as well as a few unanswered questions but it is not amazing on its own. What is amazing about _Lost_ is its ability to be quite compelling and keep you pressing that **"Next Episode"** button. Most episodes leave you in a place where you are craving what happens next. It often pulls the trick of leaving you in a high tension scenario, then having the next episode be a flashback or a different perspective on the same event before paying off the promise another episode later.

Say what you will about the "Mystery Box" approach but it makes for really good television. Lost is at its best when you have seen something you feel like you shouldn't have and then watch as you slowly get all the layers peeled back. While the initial premise of the show seems so small and contained, it gets out of hand pretty quickly and you are immersed in the infinite possibilities of what could happen next.

## The Later seasons

As I mentioned earlier, the later seasons of the show are highly criticized and are considered to be "trash" compared to the show's initial promise. In my opinion, that is completely false. The later seasons of the show are **rad**.

You should take that with a grain of salt because unlike anyone watching at the time, I grew up with vague _Lost_ spoilers in my head. I knew the show was going to add in time travel/multiple timelines. I knew about the ever-expanding cast. Most importantly, I knew to go in with an open mind. If I were watching as it happened, I have no idea what my reaction would be.

The later seasons are great because they make the viewer feel just as important as the characters. After every episode, me and my wife would discuss our little theories and point out question that needed to be addressed. Being right, wrong, or taken completely by surprise became a game for us. I was as invested in this silly story as any provided by prestige media.

## C H A R A C T E R S

_Lost_ is intriguing, fun, and completely mad at times but it is hoisted up by its characters. Sometimes, they are insanely annoying but most of the time, they feel like family. The show was able to take a character that I [really hated](https://twitter.com/PartTimePretzel/status/1449873679836385283) and turn him into [one of the very best](https://twitter.com/PartTimePretzel/status/1454954586477899784). I was not a fan of Sawyer or Josh Holloway's portrayal when the show began. He was needlessly cruel and wasn't able to properly justify his own actions with his backstory. Then, he started being really funny and genuinely cared about others. He started stealing scenes with [great lines](https://www.quotes.net/show-quote/48426). I didn't mind him as a love interest anymore. He was nice and sweet and able to treat others with respect.

Sawyer is just where it starts, the show offers a slew of characters played by actors who were no-names at the time but have gone on to do bigger things. Evangeline Lily got her start on _Lost_ as Kate Austen. She was a criminal with good intentions who made her way on the island by tracking and helping others on any adventures. Now she is in the fucking MCU. Daniel Dae Kim has a character arc similar to Sawyer. Jin starts out as an over-protective, aggressive, and misogynistic husband but slowly transforms into a warm and caring man. There are some characters like Jack Shepard (the "lead" of the show) who are kind of bland but eventually get some layers. A spinal surgeon with rage issues that lost his wife because of his constant mental unraveling makes for an interesting redemption story as well.

Andrew Naveen steals a lot of the show as Sayid Jarrah, a former member of the Iraqi Republican Guard who uses his _interesting_ interrogation skills to keep everyone honest. Naveen gets to have one of the most complete characters. We see him when he is a loyal member of the military, a hopeless romantic, a deserter, a negotiator, a lover, and eventually John Wick.

Going over all the performances would take too much time, but I have to highlight one in particular...

## An ode to Hurley

**Dude**, Jorge Garcia plays my absolute favorite character on the show: Hugo Reyes, better known as Hurley. Hurley is the definition of a ride-or-die ([sometimes literally](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uGAA5fm0nEY)). Hugo makes his way on the island by being a loyal friend, a moral compass, and a reminder that without fun, Jack becomes a dull boy (I'm sorry. I had to.)

The biggest tragedy of living in a post-_Lost_ world is that Jorge Garcia isn't a bigger star. The guy has a lot of personality and breathes character into lines that aren't always fleshed out. I think at a certain point, the writers just thought that having Hurley say "dude" a bunch was his whole character. That period was brief but Garcia managed to maintain Hurley's characteristic optimism in the face of a mountain of adversity. I hope that his career take some kind of upturn because he is a joy to watch.

## Were audiences ready for _Lost_?

A few weeks ago when I was finishing up season one, I talked to a lifetime fan of the show. They had binged it several times and held it in high regard. They told me that a lot of their friends who watched the show as it played out got increasingly disappointed in the quality and some even quit before the finale. We hypothesized that the show was better as a binge than weekly viewing (I normally have the opposite opinion but we can save that for a different post), and this caused a divide in how much people enjoyed the show.

I mentioned earlier that the show would do a set up, delay through flashback or perspective switches for a few episodes, and then pay-off. I can see how this would be frustrating for a weekly viewer. Most prestige shows only do this once or twice for dramatic effect but it is a regular occurrence on _Lost_. There are stretches of the show that I would dread to watch over the course of a month and I am glad I didn't have to.

Also, _Lost_ premiered on ABC in 2004. Network television was rarely serialized as the episodic and procedural nature of other shows was easier to digest for audiences at the time. Serialization was saved for premium [television like HBO](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0141842/?ref_=nv_sr_srsg_0). Keeping up with a wide cast of characters and a plot with so many moving parts and callbacks was very new to the territory so the show ran the risk of being misunderstood. _Lost_ is a big swing and I am glad it landed well enough to get the run that it did.

## The Finale

Before starting the series, I was dreading the finale on the grounds of the impression I got from people growing up. I knew that it was a bit open-ended and that it was not fitting for the show the preceded it. This is once again completely false. Much like the show as a whole, the finale received some unearned heat.

First of all, the finale is not ambiguous at all. We know what happened to each character (except for Michael for whatever reason) and we know where they end up. All the couples are together. Everyone is reunited. The whole show was a means to bring this assorted group of individuals together for the rest of time. They hammer it home **a lot** in the last season, but to reiterate: The show is about moving on. We are all taking part in a mortal race to find out what and who makes us happy so that we can spend our time in a way that matters.

I was not expecting the finale to hit me in the feels though. I am not a person who never cries at movie and television, but it does take something exceedingly emotional to get me to that point. The _Lost_ finale got me to cry two times. Both were moments when Hurley got to meet up with a formerly deceased member of the cast. A smile grows on his face that is so sincere and elated. I couldn't help but shed a few. I was so happy that these people got to reunite and fill each others hearts.

## Final Thoughts

In case you can't tell by now, I really love this show. Despite its flaws in storytelling and being over-long it ends up crafting a complete experience. People complain that the show jumped the shark when they added in time travel but there was a **god damn smoke monster** all the way back in season one. If you were on board with that, time travel is barely even a step up in nuttiness. _Lost_ belongs in the annals of television history. Not only was it crazy popular during its run but it has stood up against the test of time and is now seen as _better_ than it used to be.
