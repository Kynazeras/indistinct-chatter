The comedy-to-horror pipeline has been strong ever since the day Jordan Peele’s Get Out hit theaters in 2017. SInce then, Peele himself has made a few more hits while other newcomers have been cutting their teeth on the genre. _Barbarian_ is Zach Cregger’s attempt at minting his name.

_Barbarian_ starts with our protagonist Tess(portrayed by Georgina Campbell) having to make a tough decision: stay in a double-booked Airbnb with a strange man or wander the streets of Detroit alone at night and possibly ruin your chances at a dream job? This dilemma seems easy to navigate until you remember a universal truth that all women have to remind themselves of: men are dangerous. To demonstrate the weight of this decision, a large portion of the first act is dedicated to her deliberation. Her choice does not come lightly and the audience knows.

Throughout the rest of the first act, _Barbarian_’s game is simple. We wonder whether or not this guy is a creep, and the movie gives us reason to believe he is actually good. He decides to sleep on the couch and let Tess take the nice bed. He waits for her to be in the room before opening a bottle of wine. He helps her with laundry. He even ends up being a possible valuable resource for a documentary she wants to make. He checks all the boxes. So now we ask ourselves: what the hell is gonna happen next?

The other shoe doesn’t drop until the next day when Tess gets locked in the basement and finds a hidden torture dungeon. She, literally, “nope”s out of the room and warns Keith to not go down. After he does we see a really human decision by Tess. All common sense (and the inane chatter of IMdb reviews) says to get the hell out of dodge but human nature compels Tess otherwise. She knows there is danger and doesn’t want Keith to get hurt. Tess makes several decisions like this throughout the movie and it is one of my favorite parts. It’s easy to forget that characters in horror movies are also humans. There are plenty of examples of characters taking **actually** stupid and selfish actions in history. _Barbarian_ gives plausible explanations for why Tess does what she does.

_Barbarian_’s true genius shines through in the second act. After a supreme display of violence and terror, we are greeted by Justin Long and the beach-side roads of sunny California. This is where most people decided if they liked or hated the movie. The stark contrast hits you like a ton of bricks. This isn’t your grandad’s horror movie. It’s got layers. Long plays AJ, a sex pest Hollywood executive who is ousted from his latest pilot for sexually assaulting an actress. Don’t worry though, this character is not redeemed. Instead he is sent packing to liquidate properties in Detroit for his impending legal defense.

I want to take a second to highlight the most amazing and electrifying scene in recent memory. After AJ gets to his house in Detroit (the very one the rest of the movie has taken place in), he discovers the torture dungeon that sent chills up Tess’ spine and he decides to start measuring it out to add to the square footage on the listing for this house. It is bonkers and Justin Long sells it all so well. Rooms filled with cages stained by blood don’t bother him; keep on measuring. I was laughing deliriously. It is the kind of flavor that a seasoned comedy director can add with a flourish.

As mentioned earlier, the movie makes no attempt to be a “false accusations ruin lives” narrative. After 10 minutes with AJ, you know he did it and he has no feelings of remorse. This makes his undoing all the sweeter. Once AJ is taken prisoner by “The Mother” and meets Tess, he tries to play feeble and sympathetic.

The third act starts with another cutaway. We meet an old man who seems to have lived a simple life many, many years ago. He gets groceries, follows a woman home, impersonates a municipal worker, unlocks her window, and later takes her to his torture dungeon where he takes many woman to inbreed. His vile actions create The Mother and AJ gets to go face-to-face with the man who is now impossibly old. After watching a video tape of his crimes, AJ gets to be high-and-mighty about it. AJ doesn’t see himself as a rapist, so he thinks he has the high ground. The old man kills himself and AJ gets a gun.

This is yet another place where Tess gets to make a human decision. Despite not knowing AJ that well, she decides to help him after she escapes the house. In return, he shoots her twice. Once literally, when he shoots her in the stomach. Second, figuratively when he throws her off a water tower in an attempt to escape The Mother. Unluckily for him, The Mother saves Tess and then turns his head into brain puree.

_Barbarian_ is not a subtle movie. It puts in the work to subvert expectations at every move. It isn’t jump-out-of-your-seat horror and it isn’t laugh-out-loud comedy. Zach Creggor does a great job of managing these tonal shifts. I was incredibly excited while watching this movie. I felt like I was watching the beginning of a great career (don’t get me wrong, Creggor has done plenty before this. WKUK was **foundational** to my love of comedy and filmmaking). The script is both grounded in human decision-making and absolutely bug-nuts in structure. This is my biggest surprise of the year and takes the cake for “best watch of Spooktober 2022”.
