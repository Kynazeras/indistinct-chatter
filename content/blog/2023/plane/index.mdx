# The _Plane_ might crash, but this movie **soars**

2023’s _Plane_, dares to ask the audience “What if there was a plane?“. It is a tall task to stretch our imaginations to that degree but once we are _on board_, the movie is ready for _lift off_. I just wish there would have been an in-theater _safety demonstration_ before the movie because the action is intense, gut-punching, and completely twisted on all accounts. There were times when I had to cover my eyes because I truly had no idea what deranged thing I might see next. I may have paid for the whole _economy seat_ but I only needed the edge.

_Plane_’s action is placed front-and-center but its performances are no slouch. Mike Colter **becomes** Louis Gaspare. He isn’t afraid to get down-and-dirty but he has a big heart. His past as a hardened criminal lends authenticity to the atrocities he commits on screen but he doesn’t feel good about it. Gaspare has to do what he can just to stay alive and support his family. Gerard Butler is also able to steal the show. There were moments in the film where I forgot he wasn’t actually a simple airline pilot with a dead wife. Gaspare also has a dead wife by the way. There is a title card in the middle of the movie that explains that all of the compelling male characters have dead wives. It was a good reminder of what makes them so compelling.

About 45 minutes in, I realized something crucial to the footprint of this movie: It is essentially a spiritual sequel to Steven Spielberg’s film _Terminal_. In that movie, Tom Hanks (doing an incredibly believable Eastern European accent) is stuck in an airport unable to return to his home country. _Plane_ is set in a motherfucking **plane**, which is what you get inside of when you are done waiting at the **terminal**. You can’t make this up. I like to think that maybe Viktor Navorski is sitting in the back of this plane, finally living out his dreams of getting home.

You are going to need to have your _seat belt_ on for the whole movie because the tension is _upright and locked_ from the jump. _Plane_ is a _first class_ action flick that takes you from the _tarmac_ to your final destination despite having faulty _landing gear_. If your theater offer _priority boarding_, I recommend getting in that line because you might not be able to find a seat pretty soon.

**PS.** The only issues I had with the movie came from the theater itself. They waited until the very last minute to let us in to the auditorium. Then we were still waiting during all the trailers and theater ads. I was sitting in between a crying baby and a guy who was a loud sleeper. Then when the movie was over and everyone was done crying, some people decided to clap and that made it weird. Leaving the theater took _forever_ because everyone was trying to be the first one out even though it would be a lot faster if we all just went it order.
