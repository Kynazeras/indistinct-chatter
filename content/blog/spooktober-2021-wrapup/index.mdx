October always delivers as a great month to watch movies. It is weird how I could watch these movies any time of year but the vibe of "spooky season" is just so strong that it makes the viewing experience that much better. The days are getting shorter, leaves are changing color, and everything is just a little bit spookier. I hope the month treated you all well, and if you didn't get the memo [spooky season is still on](https://twitter.com/BadPostsLLC/status/1455026348171272195). I have continued watching horror movies for the time being as I still have a few left that I really want to get to. However, this post is about what I've already watched so let's jump in.

I watched A LOT more than I thought I would and some of these ended up being on whim. I had a lot of fun basically picking a random movie I hadn't seen and just pressing play. I get a lot of recommendations from [letterboxd](https://letterboxd.com/PartTimePretzel/) and YouTube that just grab my attention. I did my first deep dive on the _Halloween_ franchise that inspired me to make [this very specific ranking of the films](https://letterboxd.com/parttimepretzel/list/ranking-of-movies-named-halloween/). That led down a rabbit hole of watching Rob Zombie movies that I am still in the middle of. After that I attacked the list I mentioned in the last post. I got most of it done and made substitutions when one of the aforementioned recommendations took me by surprise.

**H A L L O W E E N**

What a picture. I discussed in my last post that John Carpenter was a master filmmaker and this movie is a prime example. It doesn't have the same level of production and horror as something like The Thing, but that is part of what makes it great. In a time when movies were either being made on huge budgets or not being made at all, Carpenter and crew made this film for $300,000 and had to use every trick in the book to get it done on budget and on time. The use of cheap props and even cheaper practical effects combined with not-so-cheap camera technology made this movie very special.

What's so great about the original Halloween is that all these factors helped John Carpenter make a horror movie with a perfect villain. The original sequels fell flat by trying to demystify Michael Myers. Instead of being evil, he was obsessed or even part of a cult. In this movie, he is just a guy who sees death as a means of punishment and can't be stopped. The new sequels (discussed below) keep in that tradition but have faults of their own. Carpenter made _Halloween_ all that it needed to be.

40 years after the original, David Gordon Greene was given the reins to retcon the all original sequels and make his 2018 film a direct sequel to the OG. He even throws in a few digs at the silly nature of those movies.

I had high hopes for this movie. A few of my favorite critics and even my best friend were over-the-moon for this installment in the series. Sadly, we can't all float on the same boat. I was unimpressed with this film. There were so many characters that were one-dimensional and disposable (Alyson's boyfriend being a painful example). Plus there is a plot twist that is handled in an overly-silly way considering the gravity of the situation. There was a ton of "dumb horror movie logic" that I can normally excuse up to a point.

There were parts that I liked though. The fact that the Laurie-Michael sibling plot was erased means that Michael can just be evil again. The movie even goes out of its way to prove that Michael has no real obsession with Laurie and that was really well done. Michael's choreography was very faithful. There were a handful of laughs but most of the attempts at comedy came out of nowhere and felt tacked-on.

This movie sucks. Pretty much every thing DGG got right in the predecessor is erased here. Michael is taking on mobs of people (including firefighters) like he is in [ _The Raid_](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1899353/). Gone is the evil being that stalks his prey, caused misdirections, and picks people off. Now he is just Dwayne Johnson. He is a brick wall that can mow people down.

Earlier I mentioned how the 2018 film had a little too much "dumb horror movie logic" but god damn does this one take it to a new level. There is a whole section of the movie dedicated to an angry mob of people led by victims of the 1978 attacks. It is supposed to be commentary on "mob mentality" but it ends up being frustrating by taking away a serious amount of this movie's (thankfully small) runtime. There are parts of this movie that would fit into DGG's other work (the Big John / Little John scenes in particular) but they just aren't Halloween material. It makes me wonder what he hopes to achieve with this new trilogy.

Speaking of trilogy, there will be another one of these stinkers named _Halloween Ends_. In a very weird move, DGG has decided he wants the next film to [address the COVID-19 pandemic](https://comicbook.com/horror/news/halloween-ends-will-acknowledge-covid-19-and-peculiar-politics/) for some reason. I have a lot of opinions about movies and television dealing with the real world pandemic that we can get to in a future blog post.

Karyn Kusama's 2009 horror/comedy deserves an apology. I am glad that in the year 2021, most have accepted that this movie fucking rules. Not only are people on film Twitter talking it up but it is also playing on [the mother fucking Criterion Channel](https://www.criterionchannel.com/jennifer-s-body). This movie features a career-best performance from Megan Fox. This is the role she was destined to play. The fact that this was panned at the time is a tragedy. We could be living in a world where Megan Fox got to do more fun and experimental genre films instead of just being objectified in big franchise blockbusters. Although, there was a [resurgence in her genre films recently](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt11804152/?ref_=nm_flmg_act_7) so there is still hope.

I want to give a specific shout out to Diablo Cody's script. After Juno, she was attached to a lot of projects and people swiftly started to turn on her. The main criticism of her work was how weird and quirky her dialogue was. Her dialogue is what gives her movies flavor. There are many ways for writers and directors to make a movie distinctly theirs, and Diablo Cody used a wide array of funny phrases, overt nicknames, and goofy metaphors to craft a world all her own.

Anyways, _Jennifer's Body_ is an absolute treat.

I am pretty sure I was the last person to watch _Squid Game_ on planet Earth. Everyone was talking about it. There were memes. People were doing their own versions. I was left out because I was busy watching _The Haunting of Hill House_ and _Lost_. So after I watched _The Haunting of Bly Manor_ I jumped on _Squid Game_.

_Squid Game_ is a part of a genre that gets exponentially more entries every year: ["Capitalism is bad"](https://www.msbreviews.com/movie-reviews/dune-spoiler-free-review). This genre is great because not only is it very honest and realistic, but it can slot itself into any other genre really easily because the horrors of capitalism are all around us. In _Squid Game_, capitalism is a game. For the rich, it is a spectator sport. For everyone else, it is a gladiator pit. This show does not shy away from the gory details of how the opportunity for wealth can cause people to act like animals. Participants don't think about how to preserve as many human lives as possible, only their own. People with good hearts are cut down by those that want the escape that wealth can afford. In the end, no one can really be happy.

Speaking of the end, it was the worst part of the show. I think there was an opportunity for a strong bookend finale but the allure of a second season was apparently too much to pass on.
